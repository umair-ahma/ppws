## Purpose
Reconcile the charter's **15 numbered domains** with the ontology's **lettered
domains** into one ratified taxonomy, so that "Domain 11" and "Domain K" never
confuse a reader. Also the master glossary of terms and acronyms. Ratified per
**Decision D-0002** (Religious & Spiritual Ecology promoted to its own domain).

## 4. Glossary of terms
- **Ontology (measurement ontology):** the master schema of *what reality the
  study measures* — domains, variables, constructs, and their causal relations —
  authored **before** any questionnaire item.
- **Domain:** a top-level construct cluster (A–P) grouping related variables.
- **Latent construct:** an unobserved variable (e.g. Agency, Wellbeing) inferred
  from multiple observed **indicators**.
- **Observed / manifest variable:** a directly recorded item or value.
- **Derived index:** a variable computed by formula from other variables (e.g.
  Caregiving Index, Economic Independence Index).
- **Mediator:** a variable on the causal path between predictor and outcome
  (Agency is the central mediator).
- **Moderator:** a variable that changes the strength/direction of a path (e.g.
  Economic Independence, Religiosity, Social Support).
- **Exogenous variable:** a variable not caused by others in the model (most of
  Domain A).
- **CORE vs OPTIONAL:** instrument-length tags — CORE items are seen by every
  respondent (~15–20 min core module); OPTIONAL items rotate via
  planned-missingness (matrix sampling).
- **Frame/quota sample:** a non-probability design recruiting from defined frames
  (professional bodies, employers, networks) under quota controls, then corrected
  with **post-stratification weighting** (D-0001).
- **Cohort:** an analytic subgroup defined by marital status (A/B above).
